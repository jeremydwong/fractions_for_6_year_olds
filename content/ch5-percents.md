# intro

Emery, I want to show you a secret disguise that fractions love to wear. It's called a **percent**, and you've seen it a hundred times — on a phone battery, on a sale sign, on a video that's *50% loaded*. Today you'll find out it was a fraction all along!

Here's the secret hidden right inside the word. **"Per cent" means "out of 100."** *Cent* is an old word for one hundred — that's why there are 100 **cent**s in a dollar, 100 years in a **cent**ury, and (almost!) 100 legs on a **cent**ipede. 🐛

So a percent is just a fraction with a bottom you never have to write down — because it's **always 100**:

- `50%` means `50/100` — fifty pieces out of a hundred.
- `25%` means `25/100`.
- `100%` means `100/100` — and you already know what *that* is... a **weirdly-written 1**! The whole thing! 🎉

The little `%` sign is just saying, *"psst — cut the whole into 100 fair pieces, and here's how many we took."*

:::callout color=cyan
A percent is a fraction in a costume. Under the `%` mask, the bottom is **always** 100.
:::end

Now here's the beautiful part — you can turn *any* fraction into a percent with our **magic 1**. We just re-cut it until the bottom is 100:

- `1/2` → multiply by `50/50` → `50/100` → **50%**. Half a pizza is 50% of a pizza!
- `1/4` → multiply by `25/25` → `25/100` → **25%**. A quarter is 25%.
- `1/10` → multiply by `10/10` → `10/100` → **10%**.

Same amount, just re-cut into hundredths. We didn't cheat — we only ever multiplied by `1`.

# outro

Do you see the clever thing the whole world did, Emery? Remember our big rule: *you can only add and compare things of the **same kind**.* So everybody secretly agreed on one favourite kind — **hundredths** — and gave it the special name **percent**. Now any two amounts can be lined up in a blink, because they're all the same kind!

That's why your phone says `100%` when it's full and `50%` when it's half-empty, why a shop sign says `25% off`, and why you might say you're *"100% sure"* you want ice cream. Every single one of those is a fraction wearing its hundredths costume.

So percents aren't a brand-new thing to learn — they're our **same-kinds** rule and our **magic 1**, all dressed up. You already knew them. 💛

— love, Daddy
