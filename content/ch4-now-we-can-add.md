# intro

Now we can put all our tricks together and do the thing that used to be impossible: **add fractions with different kinds!**

Remember our stuck problem? `1/2 + 1/4`. A half and a quarter — different sizes, can't add. But now we have *magic*. Here's the plan, three little steps:

1. **Spot the problem:** the bottoms are different (`2` and `4`). Different kinds!
2. **Use a magic 1:** turn `1/2` into quarters by multiplying by `2/2`. That gives `2/4`. Now *both* are quarters!
3. **Add the same kinds:** `2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4`. Done! 🎉

We turned apples-and-bananas into apples-and-apples, and then it was easy.

:::end

# outro

That's the whole secret of adding fractions, Emery:

**Make the kinds the same (with a magic 1), then add the tops.**

You already knew the hard part — adding the same kinds — since you were tiny, lining up ones and tens. The only new trick was using a weirdly-written `1` to make the kinds match. And that's *allowed*, because multiplying by 1 changes nothing.

Now have a go at the practice puzzles. Take your time, and remember: same bottoms first, then add the tops!

:::takehome color=orange
:::major
- To add fractions with different bottoms: first make the bottoms **the same**.
- Use a **magic 1** (like `2/2`) to re-cut a fraction without changing its amount.
- Once the kinds match, just **add the tops** and keep the bottom.
:::minor
- This is the same idea as lining up ones with ones and tens with tens.
- A good kind to aim for is one that *both* bottoms fit into.
:::end
