# intro

Hi Emery! Dad made you a little playground about **fractions**. We get to cut things up, colour them in, and press buttons. Let's play and learn at the same time!

So... what is a fraction? A fraction is just a way to talk about **part of a whole thing**.

Imagine we have a cookie or a pizza, or a cake, and we want to share it *fairly*. We cut it into **equal** pieces — every piece should be exactly the same size. That's the big rule: **the pieces have to be fair, all the same size 

(ASIDE: whoa! what's 'size'?!? haha. good question. In this case we mean just the simple idea: 'does this piece look the same size as the other piece'? Mathematically we mean the 'area' of the piece--how much crayon you'd need to colour each piece in, or how much icing you'd need to cover the top. But we can leave that idea for later! for now, if pieces have the same size, you'd equally choose one over the other =)**

A fraction is written with two numbers, one on top of the other:

- The **bottom** number says: *how many equal pieces we cut the whole thing into.*
- The **top** number says: *how many of those pieces we are talking about.*

So `1/2` means "cut it into **2** fair pieces, and take **1** of them." That's a half! And `3/4` means "cut it into **4** fair pieces, and take **3** of them."
