# intro

Wow, Emery — look how much you learned! Let's remember the big ideas, and then let's spot fractions hiding all around you.

Here are five things that I hope we got from this little practice:

- A **fraction** is part of a whole, cut into **fair, equal** pieces. Bottom = how many pieces; top = how many you take.
- Every plain number is **already a fraction** over 1 (`5` is `5/1`).
- You can only add things that are the **same kind** — just like ones and tens.
- A fraction with the same top and bottom is a **weirdly-written 1**, and multiplying by 1 changes *nothing*.
- So to add different fractions, use a **magic 1** to make the kinds match, then add the tops!

And once you know fractions, you'll start seeing them *everywhere*:

- It's **half past** three on the clock — that's `1/2` of an hour gone!
- **Quarter past** four? That's `1/4` of an hour.
- "Can I have **half** the chocolate bar?" 🍫
- Cutting a pizza into 8 slices and eating 3 — you ate `3/8`!
- A measuring cup that says `1/2` cup of flour for baking.

:::callout color=cyan
Fractions are just the language of **sharing fairly** and **parts of things**. Now that you know the words, you'll hear them all day long.
:::end
