# intro

Hi Emery! Here's a little page that will help us learn about **fractions**. We get to cut things up, colour them in, and press buttons. The main goal for this is to try to teach the hard things that kids sometimes have trouble with. 

**part of a whole thing**

Imagine we have one cookie, and we want to share it *fairly*. So we cut it into **equal** pieces — every piece exactly the same size. That's the big rule: **the pieces have to be fair, all the same size.**

A fraction is written with two numbers, one on top of the other:

- The **top** number says: *how many pieces we are talking about.*
- The **bottom** number says: *how many of those pieces make up one whoe thing.*

So `1/10` means "we have **1** piece, out of two total **10** of them.`1/2` is the simplest fraction maybe, it means "one half" in regular speech but of course the word "half" is a special case, like the number "eleven" or another fraction "quarter", just cute-fraction-names like "Re-Re" or "Epi" or even "darlin." 

:::callout color=gold
Have a go below if you like, at saying the fraction out loud. Use the cute names like "half" and "third" and "quarter" if you like! Use the buttons to **cut** the pizza into more pieces, and **colour in** the pieces you want. Watch how the fraction changes.
:::end

Try to remember: the top number is the *how many* (how many you are thinking about), and the bottom number is the *total per one*. This is sorta the second time that a bigger symbol means actually smaller: you've seen negative numbers where -$100 (you lost money, or owe someone) is in some real sense less than -$1, right? Fractions are a bit similar in that 1/100 is less than 1/10, or 1/1. We have 1 piece cut from a 100-slice pizza: what a small slice that would be! Much smaller than 1 piece cut from a 10-slice pizza (or a 1-slice pizza!).
